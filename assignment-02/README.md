# 🏢 Business Case 02 — Semantic Lead Generation Form

> **Module 01 · HTML Semantic Foundation (Deep Dive)**

Build a production-ready **Lead Generation Page** using **100% HTML5 Semantic** for an online fitness coaching business. The project focuses on creating an accessible, semantic, and user-friendly application form that works seamlessly across devices and screen readers without relying on CSS or JavaScript.

## 💼 Client Brief

### Client

**_Elevate Fitness_**

A modern fitness company expanding its online coaching services and helping clients achieve their health goals through personalized training programs.

---

### Project

**_Semantic Lead Generation Form_**

---

### Business Context

Lead generation forms are one of the most valuable assets for any online business. Every potential customer begins their journey by submitting information through a form.

The client requires a semantic HTML5 application page that is accessible, easy to navigate, and optimized for assistive technologies. The document should clearly organize form controls, improve usability, and provide a strong foundation for future frontend development.

---

## 🎯 Project Goals

The page should:

- Collect coaching applications efficiently.
- Build a fully semantic HTML5 form.
- Improve accessibility for screen readers.
- Provide a logical document hierarchy.
- Enhance usability through proper form controls.
- Prepare a scalable foundation for future styling and validation.

## 📝 Reflection

This business case demonstrates that semantic HTML extends beyond page layout—it is essential for building accessible and user-friendly forms.

By using elements such as `<form>`, `<fieldset>`, `<legend>`, `<label>`, and appropriate input controls, the application becomes easier to understand for both users and assistive technologies. This semantic foundation enhances accessibility, supports search engine understanding, and prepares the project for future styling and client-side validation.
